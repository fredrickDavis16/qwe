Favorite Steak Recipe

Want to know how to make the best steak everytime? Well this recipe is made just for you and no you do not have to be a professional chef to do this

Step 1

Steak temperatures

First you have to decide what temperature you want your steak to be cooked at. There are multiple temps to choose from like:

  • rare
  • medium rare
  • medium
  • medium well
  • well done

The most common temps are medium-rare and medium. There are a couple reasons steaks are cooked with different temperatures. When it comes to beef or more specifically steak, it doesn't have to be cooked all the way through unlike pork or chicken. Now that doesnt mean you should eat it completely raw but no you don't have to cook it all the way and no you will not get sick. Essentially, the more the steak is cooked, the less chance that the meat will contain all the juices we love to taste. Also the more it is cooked, the more chewy the meat gets and no one wants to be eating on a rubber tire. There are exceptions to these rules sometimes but for the most part these rules mostly always apply. So you can see why people tend to go for steaks around the medium area. It contains most of the juices while also being very easy to chew and swallow. So choose which temp you like the best and go from there. Mine is medium to medium well so that's what i'm going to describe in this recipe.

rare medium-rare medium medium-well well-done

Step 2

How to cook

Next you have to decide how you want your steak to be cooked. There are many ways to cook a steak but I will name the best options.

  1. cooked completely on a stove using a skillet, most commonly a cast iron
  2. cooked completely on a grill or blacktop
  3. seared using a skillet then using an oven to reach the preferred temperature
  4. cooked mostly in an oven then searing using a skillet. This is called reverse searing

In this recipe we are going to keep it simple and cook it completely on a skillet

Step 3

Gather ingredients

  • ribeye steak
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • chili powder
  • lemon pepper
  • seasoning blend if you have one you like
  • butter
  • olive oil

You can also use a meat thermometer if needed.

Step 4

Prepare the steak

Allow the steak to thaw completely out. Once the steak is completely thawed out, drizzle salt over both sides of the steak and rub the salt in the steak and let it sit for another 30 minutes. After 30 minutes it's time to season the steak. Drizzle the pepper, garlic powder, chili powder, and lemon pepper one by one over both sides of the steak. Don't over season your steak but don't under season it either. If you have your own seasoning blend you can use that instead of the seasonings I have named.

Depending on what type of meat thermometer you are using you can go ahead and stick the probe in the meat now. If you have a normal meat thermometer then you will just stick the probe in from time to time to check the temp repeatedly while the steak is cooking.

Step 5

Time to cook the steak

Turn the stove on and crank the heat almost all the way up and sit the skillet on the stove.

The higher the heat the faster it'll cook and also the better the sear. Give it about 5 minutes and you're ready to start cooking. Drizzle a little olive oil in the pan and sit the steak in the pan.

You should hear a loud sizzling sound, if not, the pan is not hot enough. There is no certain time each steak has to cook because there are too many factors that play into cooking a steak such as what cut of steak you are cooking, what type of pan you're using, is the steak frozen or thawed, and what temp are you trying to achieve. The more you cook steaks the less you will use a thermometer but there is nothing wrong with making sure your food is exactly how you want it to be. I like my steaks medium to medium well so I will cook my steak until it reaches a temp between 140 and 150 degrees. So I will cook one side of the steak until it's around 100 degrees and I will flip it over and let it cook until a little under the desired temp, so around 136.

Once you flip the steak over let it sear until around 125. Once the temp is 125 turn the heat half way down and wait a little. Then add your butter in the pan and start basting your steak until it gets right under your desired temp. You want to turn the heat down because if the pan is too hot your butter will burn and ruin the whole steak. Your butter should be a nice golden brown. If it is dark brown then you have burned the butter.

Take the steak out right under you desired temp so I will take my steak out around 135-137 degrees. It will continue to cook for a little while after you take it out, that's why you take it out a little early. Now just let the steak rest without cutting it. This resting period is letting the juices rest back inside the meat. If you cut it too soon you will notice a lot of those nice juices spill out of the steak and go to waste. A rule of thumb is to let the steak rest however long you cooked it. So if it took 5 minutes to cook then you let it rest for another 5 minutes AT LEAST.

Cooking a steak may seem like an intense process but it really doesn't take long at all and it isn't hard. Just pay attention to what you're doing.

Step 6

Enjoy

Now that your steak has rested you can finally cut and eat it. You may add anything you want from this point on whether it be hot sauce, ranch, A1 sauce, or more butter. Enjoy your nice steak!
